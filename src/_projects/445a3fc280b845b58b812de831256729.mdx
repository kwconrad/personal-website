It was may. My life was up in the air. I was struggling to find a rental in an increasingly uncertain economy while rents were steadily climbing. Checking Zillow every hour, talking with rental agencies, and of course, because I'm from the bay area, I was checking Craigslist.

For those who aren’t familiar with Craigslist, it is a free online marketplace for selling pretty much anything, for posting jobs, personal ads, etc.

So, anyway, my wife and I had been looking day in and day out, but our time was running out. In a little over a week, our airbnb stay would expire.

Then we found it! Beautiful place, affordable, clean. We instantly reached out, thinking we were being proactive. We gave contact info, and explained who we were, and waited to hear back from our amazing host, who not only had an amazing place, but had very similar taste to me and my wife.

Cut to 2 days later. “Kyle? Whats this?”. My wife shows me her phone. And its a text from “Kyle”. From a weird number. Asking questions about how shes doing, trying to impersonate "me" I guess. So our potential new landlord wasn&apos;t actually a landlord at all. They were this mysterious, nebulous thing we collectively call spam.

We had been spammed! And we werent alone.

Some quick statistics for you:

- In 2021, **319.6 billion e-mails** were sent and received on a daily basis [(Statista)](https://www.statista.com/statistics/420391/spam-email-traffic-share/)
- Spam messages accounted for **45.37 percent** of e-mail traffic in December 2021 [(Statista)](https://www.statista.com/statistics/420391/spam-email-traffic-share/)

Big issue? I think it is.

But there are solutions.

I thought to myself: this needs to stop. So I did some research.

I thought: what is spam? Its robots right? And people create it?

I realized I didn't even actually know what spam is. So I started digging and moved into some discovery around what spam is, and common ways to prevent it.

**Initial Assumptions:**

The spam on craigslist is often generated by users

**Approach:**

- Understand what "spam" is
- Research common practices for avoiding spam
- Research user interfaces for verifying or legitimizing a user.
