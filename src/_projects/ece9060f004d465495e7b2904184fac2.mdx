### **Problem statement**

Find creative people meaningful work in their region that fits their skillset, based on trust and credibility from others (ie recommendations, testimonials, previous local hires, etc.)

### **Initial chat**

In our initial chat, the client spoke about an issue where there was jobs available for creative people in the GSR(greater sac region). She receives job opportunities from friends she knew in her network and from companies looking to hire creatives, but she has no way to link these jobs to people who need them.

### **Initial assumptions about the problem**

From our initial chat, the problem seemed to be rooted in not being able to connect creative people with the range of jobs available to them in the region. The client talked about her consistent flow of job posts that she received from a lot of different places(email, slack, word of mouth, etc.), and she wanted a place to manage and organize these jobs so that creative people in the area could benefit from that.

From this, I assumed:

- We would need to build this ourselves
- This would be a UI that would live on its own experience, but as part of our website (like jobs.creativecapitolalliance.org or something similar).
- There would be a search bar
- This would be a web application
- There would be mobile support/the app would be responsive
- There would be accounts/a sign up process
- There would be tiers (some users get more exposure than others)

### **Initial research**

The initial research I did was around gathering data that exposed the current experience creative people in the sacramento region were having when looking for work.

**Initial demographic survey**

I wanted to know:

- What do they generally use in terms of applications
- What are their demographics
- How difficult is the process for them
- How do they show their work

**User Interviews**

After reviewing the survey results, I decided I needed to zoom in on the results I got back from the survey, and talk to some people about their experience. From talking to people about their experience looking for and finding work, there was some interesting findings. Some of the most interesting results I found were:

- To find jobs, we should cast a wide net. Its a numbers game, so we just need to apply as much as we can, and narrow down the responses.
- We need to rely on our network. Referrals are huge in creative work. And its a good idea to lean on your network.

**Closing initial thoughts**

The most common thread I saw within all of my initial research was the need for social proof. Having connections and experiences that justify your candidacy for a position, and being able to display that as a potential candidate.

The rest of the thinking that I did on this product focused on generating user interfaces and wireframes that explored social proof on a psychological level and attempted to make a user experience around that.
